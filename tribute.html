Abdollah Alimorad

Iranian Puppeteer & Animation Pioneer.

Biography

Abdollah Alimorad is one of Iran’s most influential masters of puppet animation, known for his delicate craftsmanship and poetic visual storytelling. Born in 1948 in Tehran, he studied animation and puppetry in Czechoslovakia, where he refined a unique aesthetic rooted in traditional Iranian art, handcrafted miniatures, and expressive puppet movement. Returning to Iran, he became a central figure at IRIB’s Institute for the Intellectual Development of Children and Young Adults (Kanoon), where he produced some of the most iconic puppet and stop-motion works in Iranian cinema. His films are known for their quiet beauty, symbolic imagery, and emotional warmth. Alimorad’s animations are not simply stories, they are worlds built by hand, where every character carries a soul and every movement feels alive.

Major Contributions

  • Founder of Iranian puppet animation with a distinct handcrafted aesthetic.
  • Produced several beloved stop-motion and puppet films for children and adults.
  • Merged traditional Iranian storytelling with European animation techniques.
  • Designed puppets with expressive, almost theatrical motion and emotional depth.
  • Inspired generations of Iranian animators, puppeteers, and visual storytellers.

Notable Works

  • “The child & The snake” (کودک و مار)
  • “Moon of the cock” (ماه خروس)
  • “The child & sea” (کودک و دریا)
  • “The city of story” puppet contributions
  • Various puppet and stop-motion shorts for Kanoon
